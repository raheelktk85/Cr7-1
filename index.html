Timeline — From Birth to Today

1985
Birth — Funchal, Madeira
Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro is born on 5 February 1985 in Funchal, on the Portuguese island of Madeira. His early years are marked by a working-class upbringing; his father, José Dinis Aveiro, worked as a kit man, and his mother, Maria Dolores, was a cook.
1997
Youth career begins
Ronaldo joins Andorinha, a local youth team, before moving to Nacional and then to Sporting CP's youth academy. His speed, skill, and ambition become evident early on.
2002
Professional debut
Cristiano makes his professional debut for Sporting CP in 2002, impressing with his dribbling and technical ability, which soon attracts top European clubs.
2003
Manchester United transfer
Alex Ferguson signs Ronaldo for Manchester United in a high-profile move. Ronaldo develops into one of the game's top wingers, helping United to Premier League titles and the 2008 UEFA Champions League.
2008
Ballon d'Or and Champions League
Ronaldo wins his first Ballon d'Or and lifts the Champions League with Manchester United (2008), establishing himself among football's elite.
2009
Real Madrid era
In 2009, Ronaldo transfers to Real Madrid for a then-world record fee. Over the following years he breaks scoring records, forms a prolific partnership with teammates, and wins multiple Champions League trophies.
2016
European Champion
Ronaldo captains Portugal to victory at UEFA Euro 2016 — the nation's first major international trophy.
2018
Juventus move
After a legendary spell at Real Madrid, Ronaldo moves to Juventus in 2018, continuing to score prolifically and adding Serie A titles to his trophy cabinet.
2021
Return to Manchester
Ronaldo makes a blockbuster return to Manchester United in 2021, reuniting with the club where he rose to stardom.
2023
Move to Al Nassr
Cristiano continues his global career by joining Saudi club Al Nassr, broadening his influence beyond Europe and helping grow the sport in new regions.
Records
All-time scoring
Across club and country, Ronaldo is among the top scorers in football history. He has broken multiple club and international scoring records, and consistently ranks among the top scorers of his generation.
Trophies
Titles & awards
Multiple league titles across countries, several UEFA Champions League trophies, multiple Ballon d'Ors, continental trophies with Portugal, and a vast number of individual accolades reflect his longevity and consistency.
Off the pitch
Philanthropy & brand
Ronaldo's influence extends into fashion, fitness, and philanthropy. He has supported hospitals, children's charities, and disaster relief efforts. His personal brand — CR7 — overlays apparel, fragrances, and more.
Legacy
Role model & influence
Ronaldo is known for extreme work ethic, athleticism, and competitiveness. He inspires many young players around the world and is one of sport’s most marketable figures.

Deep dive sections

Early life & youth

Raised in a working-class family, Ronaldo's dedication and early move to Sporting's academy set the technical and professional foundation for his career. Coaches noted his determination, dribbling flair, and hunger for improvement. He left Madeira as a teenager to pursue football in the Portuguese capital.

Manchester United — development & breakthrough

Under Sir Alex Ferguson, Ronaldo refined his game: improving crossing, decision-making, and goal threat. Evolving from a flashy winger into an elite scorer, he won domestic titles and his first Champions League.

Real Madrid — peak scoring years

At Real Madrid, Ronaldo became a goalscoring machine, competing at the very highest level across La Liga and European competition. He set numerous club records and helped Real lift multiple UEFA Champions League titles.

International success

As Portugal's captain, Ronaldo led the team to victory at UEFA Euro 2016 and later contributed to the 2019 UEFA Nations League win. He has represented his country across multiple World Cups and major tournaments.

Training & mentality

Ronaldo is known for intense physical preparation, strict diet, and recovery routines. His discipline has allowed him to maintain elite performance into his late 30s and beyond.
